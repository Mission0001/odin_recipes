How To Cook Lengua Kare Kare

This, like your regular Kare-Kare, utilizes a thick, peanut stew. But to complement it, we use beef tongue instead of other beef cuts. Why? Well, because when cooked right, beef tongue or lengua can make for the most tender and satisfying ingredient. And you’ll only be better able to savor this alongside the tasty bok choy, eggplant and other vegetables.

How To Cook Pork Adobo

Being familiar with a well-loved and popular dish often leads us to turn to the same classic recipe all of the time. After all, why fix what isn’t broken? But I say you shouldn’t turn away from a recipe upgrade to give yourself a pleasant culinary surprise every once in a while. This is why I think you should give this Pinakamasarap na Pork Adobo recipe a try.
